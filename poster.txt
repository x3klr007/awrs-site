Aqua Well Robot Solution (AWRS)
Single descent diagnostics: 360° imaging, in site water quality, casing integrity, gas / liquid leaks detection, multi depth water sampling

IP Application No. SA 1020252223
Supported by Energy & Water Academy and Tuwaiq Academy

Problem Statement:

Solution:

Groundwater wells are often monitored with
outdated rope-and-bailer tools, giving limited
insight into groundwater condition and wellbore
integrity.
These methods miss depth-dependent waterquality changes and multi-depth aquifer
chemistry.
They can overlook early casing corrosion,
structural damage, and toxic gas/chemical leaks.
Worldwide, many wells are already contaminated
or unreliable, increasing the need for better
monitoring.
Without real-time, multi-depth monitoring, issues
may go unnoticed until failures, risking water
safety and raising costs.

AWRS is an integrated smart
well-inspection platform that
combines multiple structural,
chemical, and environmental
sensors into one automated
device deployed in a single
descent. It delivers complete realtime diagnostics in one operation.

Results
Approach Validation: The results confirmed the success of using
Machine Learning to decode the
Electrochemical Impedance Spectroscopy (EIS) data. The highest
accuracy achieved was 96% (using the
RFC model), which fulfils high-accuracy spectrum classification.
Model Selection: Linear (LR) and basic (SVC) models failed to
achieve the required accuracy (54% 71%). In contrast, Ensemble Methods, especially Random Forest,
demonstrated a superior capability to
handle the nature of EIS data, indicating that the relationship
between the extracted features and the
concentration is non-linear and complex.
